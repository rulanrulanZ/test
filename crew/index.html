CREW

Crew is a concept app that helps you filter employees within your company by capabilities. Whether you're curious of what others can do or want to find the right team member for your next project, Crew makes filtering candidates by skills easier than ever! Create your profile, add your skills, and discover or wait to be discovered.

You can view the prototype here.

Problem

Project leads form teams based on who they know or who they have worked with before. Most likely employees within large companies are unaware of the unique capabilities their colleagues possess.

Solution

Crew bridges the gap between employees and their colleagues so they can gain insight into what skills and capabilities people have within the company. This can help form better teams by rallying people with the most appropriate capabilities for every project.

Prime Objective

The primary goal of this app is to give users the ability to filter through colleagues easily and fast. If this app is going to be used as a resource for starting a team project, I have to make it the least stressful and time consuming part of the project.

My Process

As Maga Design began to grow, not only did we have more projects but an influx of new team members. One day I noticed that project leads were forming teams with the same colleagues. I assumed it was because they didn't know how others could contribute, otherwise they would just be playing favorites. That's when I thought of the idea.

"If someone wanted to find a colleague with a certain skill for either a project or just out of curiousity an app should be able to help them with that."

And thats how Crew was inspired. Since skills are ever-changing and becomming more technical, I designed an interface that was modern and sleek.

I dedicated one weekend to:

  1. Figuring out the information architecture
  2. Sketching out wireframe ideas for the two main interactions of the tool: filtering employees by skill and viewing employee profiles. The function of the app was to simply allow users to select skills to filter employees within their company.
  3. Converting sketches into lo-fi mockups
  4. Sharing mockups with family and friends
  5. Designing hi-fi mockups
  6. Making revisions, sharing again, making more revisions
  7. Building prototype in Invision
